# WORLDr — World Foundation Canon

## Overview

The world of WORLDr is called **Aethon** — a fictional planet with four inhabited continents, each with distinct geopolitical histories, economic structures, and governance cultures. Aethon is roughly analogous to Earth but diverged at a fictional point in history approximately 300 years ago.

---

## The Four Continents of Aethon

---

### 1. ALDERIS
*The Old World — Europe-Inspired*

**Geographic Identity**
- Northwestern peninsula continent
- Temperate maritime west, continental center, alpine south
- Dense urban clusters separated by river valleys and farmland
- Climate: Maritime (west), Continental (center), Alpine (south), Mediterranean (far south)

**Short History**
Alderis was home to the ancient Valdren Empire, which collapsed 800 years ago, fracturing into dozens of city-states and kingdoms. Over centuries of wars, trade, and diplomacy, these consolidated into modern nation-states. The Great Reform Era (200–150 years ago) saw most monarchies transition to constitutional systems following the "Velvet Revolutions" — bloodless parliamentary takeovers enabled by industrialization and educated middle classes. Alderis industrialized first among the continents, establishing its economic and institutional dominance.

**Political Identity**
- Strong parliamentary tradition
- Constitutional monarchies and parliamentary republics dominate
- Coalition politics is the norm — single-party majorities are rare
- High institutional trust; independent judiciaries, free press, strong civil services
- Slow-moving but highly stable governance

**Economic Characteristics**
- Post-industrial service economies with advanced manufacturing niches
- High wages, high taxes, strong welfare states
- Aging populations, declining birth rates
- Export-driven: machinery, pharmaceuticals, chemicals, financial services
- High household wealth, moderate growth (2–3% annually)

**Cultural Identity**
- Emphasis on civic duty, education, and institutional deference
- Multi-generational political families; politics as a respectable career
- Strong regional identities within nations (dialects, customs, local parties)
- Conservative social change pace but strong minority rights frameworks

**Geopolitical Tendencies**
- Prefer multilateral diplomacy over unilateral action
- Founding members of the Alderis Common Council (ACC) — regional economic bloc
- Generally pacifist; large military budgets but rarely deployed

**Demographic Tendencies**
- 65+ age group: 22–28% of population
- Birth rates: 1.4–1.7 (below replacement)
- High immigration from Kethara and Varanthos
- Education rates among highest in Aethon

**Playable Nations (Alpha):**
- **Keldoria** — Constitutional Monarchy, manufacturing powerhouse *(starter nation)*
- Valdenmoor *(future)*
- Rhenmark *(future)*
- Solvenia *(future)*

### 2. VARANTHOS
*The Rising East — Asia-Inspired*

**Geographic Identity**
- Vast eastern continent stretching from arctic tundra to tropical archipelagos
- Dominated by river deltas, mountain ranges, and fertile plains
- Climate: Tundra (north), Continental (center), Subtropical (south), Tropical (archipelago southeast)

**Short History**
Varanthos was home to three ancient civilizations — the Karvesh Empire in the north, the Sunling Confederation in the center, and the Archipelago Leagues of the south. Colonial expansion from Alderis in the 1600s–1800s reshaped borders and economies. Independence movements swept Varanthos 80–100 years ago, creating a patchwork of new states. Some retained strong central governments inherited from colonial administration; others collapsed into civil war. Today, Varanthos is the fastest-growing economic region on Aethon.

**Political Identity**
- Highly varied: one-party states, presidential republics, semi-presidential hybrids
- Strong executive culture; parliaments often advisory in practice
- Ruling parties frequently dominant for decades
- Technocratic governance becoming fashionable in wealthier city-states
- Filial and hierarchical political culture; loyalty over ideology

**Economic Characteristics**
- Global manufacturing center; most Aethon consumer goods produced here
- Chaebol-style industrial conglomerates control major sectors
- Rapid urbanization: 60% urban (up from 30% 50 years ago)
- High savings rates; low but rising consumption
- Currency management and export subsidies are standard policy tools

**Cultural Identity**
- Confucian-adjacent collective values: family, education, hierarchy
- Intense educational competition; exam culture dominates youth politics
- Regional diversity masked by centralized national narratives
- Rising youth frustration with stagnant social mobility

**Geopolitical Tendencies**
- Bilateral deal-making; resist multilateral binding agreements
- Economic influence expansion through infrastructure investment abroad
- Several nations actively competing for regional hegemony

**Demographic Tendencies**
- Enormous working-age populations (15–64: 68–72%)
- Birth rates falling rapidly in wealthier nations (1.2–1.5)
- Internal migration from rural to megacities (50M/year continent-wide)
- Rapidly growing middle class (800M+ projected by 2040)

### 3. NOVARA
*The New World — Americas-Inspired*

**Geographic Identity**
- Double-continent landmass connected by narrow isthmus
- Northern Novara: temperate to boreal; Southern Novara: tropical to Patagonian
- Climate: Arctic (far north), Temperate (north center), Tropical (equatorial belt), Arid (southern plains), Polar (far south)

**Short History**
Novara was first settled by maritime peoples from Alderis and Varanthos 400 years ago, displacing indigenous civilizations. Northern Novara declared independence 200 years ago and rapidly industrialized under a federal presidential republic model. Southern Novaran nations achieved independence 150–80 years ago, with recurring cycles of democratic government and military coups. Today, Northern Novara is the world's largest economy by GDP; Southern Novara contains the world's highest inequality and fastest-growing youth populations.

**Political Identity**
- Presidential and semi-presidential republics dominate
- Extreme media influence on politics — television and now digital media shape elections
- Populism is endemic; charismatic leaders over institutions
- Northern Novara: mature democracy with partisan gridlock
- Southern Novara: fragile democracies, frequent elections, volatile coalitions

**Economic Characteristics**
- Northern Novara: advanced post-industrial, tech-driven, reserve currency issuer
- Southern Novara: commodity exporters (oil, copper, soybeans), high informality
- High inequality (Gini 45–55 in south); middle class squeezed
- Strong private sector; weak public services in south
- Northern Novara exports capital and culture globally

**Cultural Identity**
- Immigrant-nation identity: diversity as national value (north)
- Tradition, religion, and family as political currency (south)
- Sports culture as political arena; elections treated as sporting events
- Strong civil society and NGO culture (north); patronage networks (south)

**Geopolitical Tendencies**
- Northern Novara: global hegemon aspirations; interventionist history
- Southern Novara: non-alignment traditions, resentment of Northern Novaran dominance
- The Novara Common Market (NCM) partially integrates the two halves

**Demographic Tendencies**
- Northern Novara: 340M, stable, aging but immigration-sustained
- Southern Novara: 680M, growing rapidly, median age 26
- Youth unemployment in south: 25–35%

### 4. KETHARA
*The Frontier — Africa-Inspired*

**Geographic Identity**
- Massive central continent, second-largest by land area
- Sahara-analogue great desert in north; vast savanna center; tropical rainforest equatorial; southern highlands
- Climate: Desert (north), Arid savanna (north-center), Tropical savanna (center), Rainforest (equatorial), Highland temperate (south), Mediterranean (far south)

**Short History**
Kethara contains humanity's oldest civilizations — the Khemri Empire in the north and the Akshari Kingdom in the east date to 4,000 years ago. Alderis colonial powers divided Kethara into 47 administrative zones 200–100 years ago, drawing borders that ignored ethnic and tribal boundaries. Independence came in waves 80–40 years ago. The legacy of extraction — resources exported with minimal local processing — still defines most economies. Some nations have broken this cycle through strong resource nationalism and industrialization policy; others remain trapped in "resource curse" dynamics.

**Political Identity**
- Enormous diversity: functioning democracies, hybrid states, military juntas, dominant-party republics
- Post-colonial institutions often underresourced and politically captured
- Strong ethnic and regional political identity; national party coalitions are fragile
- Young, growing civil society challenging traditional elites
- Some nations among Aethon's most vibrant democracies; others among least

**Economic Characteristics**
- Resource-driven: oil, minerals, rare earths, agricultural commodities
- High growth potential (6–9% in well-governed nations) but volatile
- Large informal economy (40–60% of GDP in most nations)
- Rapidly growing tech and mobile-first startup ecosystems
- Infrastructure gap: roads, power, internet — largest investment opportunity on Aethon

**Cultural Identity**
- Enormous linguistic diversity (2,000+ languages continent-wide)
- Pan-Ketharan identity movements gaining political traction
- Strong oral and community governance traditions alongside formal institutions
- Youth population: largest youth bulge on Aethon — 60% under 25

**Geopolitical Tendencies**
- Non-alignment tradition; avoid great power entanglements
- Ketharan Union (KU) — weak regional body, growing economic relevance
- Resource-diplomacy: nations leverage natural resource access for infrastructure deals

**Demographic Tendencies**
- 1.4 billion and growing rapidly; median age 19
- Urbanization at 45%, rising 2% annually
- Education gaps closing rapidly with mobile-first learning
- Diaspora remittances: 8–15% of GDP in many nations

## The Alderis Common Council (AUC)

The only active supranational body implemented in Alpha v0.1. The AUC is a regional economic bloc of Alderis nations sharing:
- Common trade standards
- Mutual investment treaties
- Shared diplomatic missions
