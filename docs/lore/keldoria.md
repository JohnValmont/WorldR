## Political History

Keldoria's modern politics began with the Veldenmoor Compact of 612 AE, which transformed an absolute monarchy into a constitutional one. King Karl II, facing industrialist-backed worker uprisings, calculated that controlled democratization was preferable to revolution. The Compact granted:
- Universal male suffrage (extended to all adults in 701 AE)
- An elected Bundestag (parliament) with legislative supremacy
- A Chancellor accountable to parliament, not the Crown
- The monarch as ceremonial head of state with defined veto powers (rarely used)

Two dominant parties emerged from the 19th century: the **Social Democrats (precursor to KSD)** representing industrial workers, and the **Christian Conservatives (precursor to CCU)** representing rural communities, industrialists, and the church. Their alternating governments defined the 20th century.

Post-war reconstruction (750–800 AE) saw the welfare state built and the two-party system fractured by the rise of the Greens, Liberals, and later nationalist and technocratic movements. Today, no single party commands a majority; coalition governments of 2–3 parties are standard.

## Constitutional Monarchy Structure

### The Crown
| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Monarch** | King Albrecht III, House Veldren, age 64 |
| **Queen Consort** | Queen Mirela |
| **Heir** | Crown Prince Edvard, age 38 |
| **Royal Motto** | *"In Service, Not Rule"* |
| **Ceremonial Powers** | Formally appoints Chancellor after coalition formation; signs laws into effect; dissolves parliament (only on Chancellor's advice) |
| **Real Powers** | None — purely ceremonial by convention and law |
| **Public Role** | National unity symbol; opens parliament; state visits; military Commander-in-Chief (nominal) |
| **Approval Rating** | High (74%) — monarchy broadly popular across parties |

### The Parliament (Bundestag der Keldoria)
| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Type** | Unicameral (with advisory Bundesrat for regional affairs) |
| **Total Seats** | 450 |
| **Election System** | Mixed-member proportional (MMP) |
| **Term Length** | 48 months (4 years) |
| **Dissolution** | Possible by Chancellor vote of no confidence + royal assent |
| **Quorum** | 226 seats (50%+1) |
| **Law Passage** | Simple majority (226+) for standard; 301+ for constitutional changes |

### Government Formation
1. Election held → seats allocated by D'Hondt PR
2. Largest party leader attempts coalition formation (21 days)
3. If failed, second-largest attempts (14 days)
4. If failed, snap election called
5. Coalition agreement signed → Chancellor proposed → Bundestag vote → King appoints

4. **Immigration Integration** — 14% immigrant population; integration programs politically contested
